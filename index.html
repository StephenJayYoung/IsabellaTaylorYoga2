About


When I had my first experience with yoga about five years back, I walked out of the Bikram studio in almost a nauseous state of absolute bliss. I hated standing on my mat in that 100+ degree room for ninety minutes while trying to hold my foot over my head, almost as much as I began to hate the monotony of the poses. But there was always an aspect of the practice that kept me coming to class.

I started branching out in my practice with different styles of yoga, and the awareness that yoga reveals to us when we can get out of our heads and into our bodies really started to slap me in the face (in the best way). I began to feel fluidity and grace within my body and mind, and the practice became a therapeutic experience. It began to aid me in understanding my own strength and beauty, as well as understanding that if you love yourself in the most genuine sense, then you can truly love others. It's an everyday practice through ups, downs, and everything in between-- but that's the beauty of it. Understanding that no matter wherever the hell you are, yoga only asks us to come back into the moment and just breathe.

In 2013, immediately after graduating high school in Lynnwood, Washington, I stumbled upon Yoyoyogi's Power Yoga Teacher Training and went for it! I moved to Portland and received my 200-RYT training that November.

My teaching is a direct connection to my own personal home practice. Being able to try and help my students flow within their bodies and let yoga take them over the way it does with me really is a magical experience-- and I am honored to share that with all of my students! Yoga helps me discover something new about myself daily when I am able to connect with my practice, and my goal as a teacher is to help my students discover the ever changing strength in their body and minds as well.

I now teach regularly at Vibrant Studios in downtown SW Portland every Monday and Wedneday morning at 7am. I also sub classes randomely, which you can find out about on my Facebook page!
